Surveillance Capitalism

The use of monitoring peoples online behaviors and using the data as monetization to sell to big tech companies for their marketing. With online platforms having a major contribution on how the modern world operates, it is beneficial for companies to use the growing trend to boost their economy. Companies use this advancement to exploit its userâ€™s online behaviors such as their search history, recent locations, sites visited, images captured, and even how the way their mouse moves and clicks throughout the screen. Having knowledge to these information, companies can make more This poses a serious threat to all the users of the platform as it invades their privacy. These companies also hold personal information that only individuals must possess such as their passwords, email addresses, bank account and bank pins, information that only individuals must have knowledge of. By requiring such information as a way to create an account for said platforms, companies get ahold of their valuable data. These data can be easily sold by companies to other bigger companies that would like to obtain them to further boost their popularity and in turn, profits.

Alternatives

Browser Pros Cons
Brave
  • Heavy privacy and security focus
  • Brave Rewards (for optionally watching ads)
  • Fast Browsing
  • Customizable UI
  • Resource Intensive
  • Limited Extension Support
  • Compatibility Issues
Arc
  • Heavy privacy and security focus
  • High Performance
  • Minimalist U.I.
  • Vertical Sidebar
  • A.I. Assistant (macOS only)
  • Requires a log-in to use
  • May run slow on some devices
  • Can be hard to get used to
Tor (The Onion Router)
  • Extremely privacy and security focused
  • No cookies by default
  • Slow speeds
  • Some websites block Tor
Firefox
  • Privacy and security focused
  • Customizability
  • High speed
  • Resource Intensive
  • Compatibility Issues
Search Engines Pros Cons
DuckDuckGo
  • Free
  • Heavy privacy and security focus
  • Own search index
  • A.I. Assist
  • No personalization (because it doesn't keep data)
  • Still has ads, but at least it's not targeted
Brave Search
  • Free
  • Heavy privacy and security focus
  • Own search index
  • A.I. Assist
  • Collects analytics by default (can be turned off)
  • Still has ads, but at least it's not targeted
Kagi
  • Very fast compared to other engines
  • Own search index
  • The most privacy and security focused
  • No ads
  • Strong A.I. assist
  • Subscription-based (starts from $5/month)
Email Pros Cons
ProtonMail
  • Free (has premium version though)
  • End-to-end encrypted
  • Extremely privacy and security focused
  • Hide my email feature
  • Extra email addresses
  • Only 1GB of free storage
  • Premium tiers start at $10/month
Tutanota
  • Free (has premium version though)
  • End-to-end encrypted
  • Extremely privacy and security focused
  • Hide my email feature
  • Extra email addresses
  • Only 1GB of free storage
  • Premium tiers start at $3/month
Messaging Apps Pros Cons
Signal
  • By a non-profit organization
  • Open-source
  • End-to-end encryption (mandatory)
  • Does not log IP addresses
  • Free
  • Registration requires a phone number
Session
  • End-to-end encryption
  • Does not require phone number or email to register
  • Open-source
  • Does not log IP addresses and metadata
  • Free
  • No 2FA
  • Connecting with others may be hard because of the unique identifier
Threema
  • End-to-end encryption
  • Does not require phone number or email to register
  • GDPR Compliant
  • Does not log IP addresses or metadata
  • Paid
  • No 2FA
  • Small user count
